Assembly Instructions

Step-by-step guide to building the physical drone frame and configuring the embedded electronics.

1. Chassis Preparation & Printing

Download the CAD files and print the 4 legs, drone body, top plate, and 4 propeller guards using PLA.

Slide the legs into the drone body. Drill one drywall screw through the holes on the body and legs (either top or bottom hole; use both if extra security is warranted) to lock them rigidly in place.

2. ESC & Motor Wiring (Crucial Step)

You must test motor rotation direction before final soldering. Pass the 3 motor wires through the holes of the ESC before soldering. If a motor spins the wrong direction, simply swap any two of the three wires to reverse it.

Use this test script on your ESP32 to verify direction via jumper cables:

#include <Servo.h>

Servo esc;

void setup() {
  esc.attach(9); // Attach ESC to digital pin 9
  Serial.begin(9600);
  
  // Arm the ESC
  esc.writeMicroseconds(1000); 
  Serial.println("Send 1000 to arm. Connect battery now.");
  delay(7000);
  Serial.println("ESC armed! Enter a value between 1100 and 1900.");
}

void loop() {
  if (Serial.available() > 0) {
    int throttle = Serial.parseInt();
    if (throttle >= 1100 && throttle <= 1900) {
      esc.writeMicroseconds(throttle);
      Serial.print("Setting throttle to: ");
      Serial.println(throttle);
    }
  }
}

Note: Ensure you wrap electrical tape around bare metal on the ESCs to prevent shorts.

3. Mounting the Propulsion System

Attach the motors and propeller guards using the M3 12mm screws. The propeller guard goes on top of the leg, and the motor goes on top of the guard, screwing directly into the holes on the bottom of the motor. Tighten securely.

IMPORTANT: Firmware Orientation

The flight controller code dictates specific motor rotations. Your physical wiring must match this exactly:
• M1 (Front Left): Counter-Clockwise (CCW)
• M2 (Front Right): Clockwise (CW)
• M3 (Rear Right): Counter-Clockwise (CCW)
• M4 (Rear Left): Clockwise (CW)

4. Flight Controller & IMU Wiring

Place the ESP32 into a breadboard (you may need two small ones bridging together so all pins are accessible). Solder jumper cables to the ESC signal wires (Black and White) to allow stable connections to the ESP32.

  • ESC Signal Pins: Pin 4 (FL), Pin 5 (FR), Pin 6 (RR), Pin 7 (RL).
  • Grounds: Route all ESC signal grounds to a shared ESP32 ground pin.

LSM9DS1 IMU Setup: Attach the IMU to the breadboard. The X forward arrow must point to the front of the drone (indicated by the horizontal slit at the front of the frame).

  • VIN -> ESP32 3.3v output
  • Ground -> Dedicated ESP32 Ground (separate from ESC grounds)
  • SDA -> Pin 8
  • SCL -> Pin 9

5. Power Distribution Hub

Place the LiPo battery in the middle compartment. Strip the wires for the male T-Plug connector, the UBEC, and the four ESCs.

  1. Twist all positive wires together and solder them uniformly.
  2. Twist all ground wires together and solder them uniformly.
  3. Solder the UBEC output to the ESP32 VIN and Ground.

Cover all soldered junctions heavily in electrical tape. Ensure all wires are tucked securely inside the central frame hub.

6. Flashing Firmware & Calibration

Connect the ESP32 to your computer via USB. Open the Arduino IDE.

  • Install the ESP32 Board Manager in preferences.
  • Install required libraries: ESP32Servo, Wire, and your preferred LSM9DS1 library.
  • Update the code with your WiFi SSID and Password.
  • To Flash: Hold the BOOT button on the ESP32, tap the RESET button, release BOOT, and hit Upload in the IDE.

Once uploaded, open the Serial Monitor. Note the IP address printed—you will paste this into your browser to control the drone.

ESC Calibration

WARNING: Ensure propellers are REMOVED before calibration.
To calibrate, your flight controller must send a maximum throttle signal (usually 2000us) as the ESCs boot. Power on the drone. The ESCs will play a musical tone. Immediately trigger the code to drop the signal to minimum (1000us). You will hear confirmation beeps indicating the throttle range is saved.

Attach the propellers matching the CW/CCW diagram, and you are ready to fly!
